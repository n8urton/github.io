Encypted Cross Platform Notes

2019-09-08
#pgp  #org  #encryption 

Challenges

I’ve tried multiple note taking solutions, including OneNote, Dropbox, Quip, GDrive, Evernote, .txt files, cherrytree, and others. They all work great in some ways, fall short in others. None work platform independent.

Requirements

Portability

  • Accessible from any machine with or without a browser
  • No service, software, or OS lock in
  • Configure what notes are synced and what notes are only accessible locally

Private

  • I shouldn’t have to agree to have them indexed by a 3rd party
  • End to end encryption

Nice to haves

  • vi keybindings
  • Rich formatting
  • Export to different formats, including markdown and pdf
  • Same environment on any workstation
  • Works offline

Enter Spacemacs + Org Mode + GnuPG

Spacemacs is emacs pre-configured with a host of plug-ins, including emacs-evil, and extensible vi layer that add vi keybindings. Org Mode is a personal information management and outlining tool for Emacs that is only limited by the time you’re willing to spend learing it. GnuPG is a message format defined by RFC4880 supporting symetric and strong public-key cryptography. Org Mode supports OpenPGP out of the box. Just save files with the .gpg extension, add your keys to your Org Mode config, and you’re set. The .gpg files can then be synced via your service of choice. GnuPG and Spacemacs are available for Windows, Mac, & Linux. Syncing with rsync even allows for use from the command line in a cloud server
